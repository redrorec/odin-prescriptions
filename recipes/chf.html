Congestive Heart Failure

Current management protocol according to the latest guidelines in an easy and digestible presentation for clinicians

First, clnically identify the usual symptoms of acute cardiac failure. Then solicit labs (Hb, Cr, Na+, K+), arterial gasometry, lactate, BNP, PCR, ECG, chest RX and ecochardiogram if available.

These first initial steps should not precede vital sign stabilization (the ABCDEs), of course, but they form the basis to the identification of the 4 subtypes of CHF.

Initial interventionm (hypertensive pulmonary edema)

  • Target MAP >60 mmHg
  • NPO until improvement of dyspnea and ROC.
  • Peripheral IVs.
  1. Start with Furosemide (20mg/mL) 0,5-1 mg/kg IV until improvement of congestion.
  2. Morphine (10mg/mL) 1 amp + 9 mL of FS 0.9% (1mg/mL). Administer 2-5 mg IV every 5-30 minutes until improvement of symptoms.
